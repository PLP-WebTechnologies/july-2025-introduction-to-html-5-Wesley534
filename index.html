Overview

The "Big Five" refers to the five most iconic and sought-after animals in African safaris: lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros. Originally a term used by big-game hunters, today it symbolizes Kenya’s rich wildlife and conservation success.

Lion

The African lion is known for its strength, social structure, and powerful roar. Lions are often seen in Maasai Mara, especially during the wildebeest migration.

Leopard

Elusive and solitary, leopards are masters of camouflage. They are often spotted resting on tree branches in Samburu and Tsavo National Parks.

Elephant

African elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. Amboseli National Park is famous for its large elephant herds with Mount Kilimanjaro as a backdrop.

Buffalo

The African buffalo is known for its unpredictable nature and strong herd bonds. Buffalo can be found in nearly every major park, especially Lake Nakuru and Nairobi National Parks.

Rhinoceros

Kenya is home to both black and white rhinos. Conservancies like Ol Pejeta are at the forefront of rhino conservation, especially for the endangered northern white rhino.
